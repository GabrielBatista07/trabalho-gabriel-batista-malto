Types of Maltodextrin


Low Molecular Weight Maltodextrin:

This form of maltodextrin is produced through a partial starch hydrolysis process. This breaks down starch chains into smaller fragments, resulting in maltodextrin with a lower molecular weight. As a result, it is rapidly digested and absorbed by the body. This makes it a popular choice for athletes and high-intensity exercisers as it provides quickly available energy. It is often used in sports drinks, energy gels, and pre-workout supplements.

High Molecular Weight Maltodextrin:

Unlike the low molecular weight variety, high molecular weight maltodextrin is produced through a more extensive hydrolysis process, resulting in larger starch fragments. This makes it slower to digest and absorb. Consequently, high molecular weight maltodextrin provides a more gradual and sustained release of energy. It is often used in products intended to provide long-lasting energy, such as some protein bars or post-workout supplements.
